Find Your Local Electronic Authority

If you have any questions or concerns about the online voting system, or if you need assistance with voter registration, here are some ways to find your local electronic authority:

1. Government Websites

Visit the official website of your country's government or election commission. Look for a dedicated section on elections or voting, where you can find contact information for the relevant authority.

2. Voter Helpline

Many countries have dedicated voter helplines or hotlines that provide assistance and support to voters. Check your country's election commission website or contact your local electoral office to find the helpline number.

3. Local Electoral Offices

Find the nearest local electoral office or election commission office in your area. They can provide you with the necessary information and guidance regarding the online voting system and any other election-related queries.

4. Online Support Portals

Some countries offer online support portals or help desks specifically for election-related queries. These portals allow you to submit your questions or concerns electronically and receive assistance from the relevant authority.

Remember, it's important to reach out to the authorized sources to ensure that you receive accurate and up-to-date information about the online voting system and any related matters.
